WHAT V5 ADDS — THREE LATTICES, TWO METRICS, ONE OS

Authority latticeSecond lattice. Distinct from read lattice. Per-org configurable: dollar ceilings per decision class, per-project authority overrides, escalation rules. Routes The One Thing to the right decider — not always the owner.
Altitude classes L0–L4L0 ops · L1 tactical · L2 functional/BD · L3 strategic · L4 existential. Every One Thing entry tagged. Altitude shift over time = the capital metric.
Multi-agent directionOne Right Hand per role. All coordinate through the Blackboard. Owner's RH brokers escalations; PM's RH stays in PM's lane. V1 ships single-RH; multi-agent is V2.0+ but V1 schema supports it without migration.
Decision router (V1 schema)Class + magnitude + assigned-decider rules → lands in that decider's One Thing. Authored in V1 even though only one role exists in V1 — schema first, surface later.
Owner's One Thing migratesL0 in early days (operational). L2 by month 9 if delegation matures. L3-L4 at enterprise scale. Volume stays roughly constant; class moves up the stack.
Two new V1 schema fieldsdecision.altitude (L0–L4) and decision_class (free-text subtype). Adds 0.5 day to W0. Enables capital metric and authority routing both.

◈ BLACKBOARD · single source of truth · permission-aware reads
Decision flowing UP the lattice: PM's RH detects a $40k scope expansion. PM's authority ceiling is $5k. RH does not notify the PM with "please escalate" — it hands the decision to the owner's RH. Owner's RH evaluates: class = scope expansion, magnitude = $40k, altitude = L1. Lands it as the owner's One Thing or batches into The Pulse depending on owner's signal preferences.
Decision flowing DOWN the lattice: Owner approves "swap Lewis sub for Moore panel pull." Owner's RH writes the approval to Blackboard. Carlos's RH (PM) surfaces the change in his Pulse. Jose's RH (super) surfaces the new vendor in his Who's Where. Lewis's RH (sub) surfaces the new PO in his lane. One write, four scoped reads. No notifications, no Slack pings — just role-scoped projections.
