RIGHT HAND · BLACKBOARD ACCESS LATTICE
Reads scoped at the persistence layer, not the UI. Each cell answers: when this role queries the Blackboard, what do they get back?

1 · Never written, not just hidden Operator-private entries are never persisted to non-owner role indices in the first place. There is nothing to leak from a cached client because the client view never received it.
2 · Read audit on every query Actor + role + timestamp logged on every Blackboard read. Same legal-defensibility chain as the De Wall capture pattern. Discoverable, exportable.
3 · Token revoke + cache purge on offboarding Departure event triggers role-token revoke and on-device cache scope-purge. Field app on a former employee's phone goes blank within sync window — no historical data carries.
4 · Cache scope = role scope Field/sub devices cache only their lane. Owner device caches everything. Enforced at the persistence layer's local mirror, not at the UI render layer.
5 · Right Hand inherits the caller's role When the PM asks Right Hand a question, Right Hand answers from the PM's read scope — not the owner's. The agent cannot launder operator-private content into a role that wouldn't see it directly.
6 · Owner can grant temporary read Owner can elevate a role for a specific entry or project window with logged justification. Elevation expires automatically. There is no permanent role escalation path.
