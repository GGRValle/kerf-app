F-SL3 · Spec

Action-led deal detail

  • Lead with state synthesis · header + RH summary + 3 signals BEFORE tabs
  • Primary action card · the highest-impact next step renders prominently above the tab strip · green-accent · clear CTA
  • Tabs are lenses · Overview default · Activity · Proposal · Notes · Files
  • No transcript/audit nav · trust surfaces stay exception-tier

Why Primary action above the tab strip

Deal detail is action-oriented in a way client/project detail isn't. The operator opens a deal to do something with it — send · follow up · reply · close. Surfacing the next action card above tabs makes the surface execution-first. Same lesson F-PR4 applied with the execution row.

5 tabs (lenses into one deal)

  • Overview (default): scope summary · pricing breakdown · linked relationships
  • Activity: chronological touches (calls · meetings · site visits · emails · RH suggestions)
  • Proposal: proposal lifecycle view (when one exists) · drafted → sent → signed
  • Notes: RH-aware notes + voice memos about the deal
  • Files: survey photos · concept sketches · selections · signed docs

Locked Sales canon applied

  • Pipeline-first, proposal-enabled, relationship-aware, pre-deal only.
  • Proposal exists in service of a deal · "View proposal" routes to F-PV1 (client-facing artifact)
  • Client = contracting counterparty · Patel-direct deal: Anjali is client, realtor Cassandra is referral source
  • Relationships block surfaces both linked parties · canonical record lives in Team+Ops > Clients

Routes

  • Back arrow → F-SL1 Sales pipeline
  • Send proposal CTA → V18 gate (external send) · post-send routes to Proposal tab
  • Preview CTA → F-PV1 Proposal Preview (client view)
  • "View proposal →" link in Pricing block → F-PV1
  • Activity tab → activity timeline · touch log
  • Anjali rel-block tap → Team+Ops > Clients > Anjali Patel (canonical record)
  • Cassandra rel-block tap → Team+Ops > Clients > Cassandra Reyes (relationship record)
  • 🎤 Speak (long-hold) → RH conversation pre-bound to Patel deal context

What this surface refuses to be

  • Not a contact record with every field visible
  • Not a parallel proposal management surface
  • Not a tabbed forensics console
  • Not Salesforce / HubSpot opportunity detail
  • Not a "rebuild the deal from scratch" form

What this surface enforces Deal state, then action. The synthesis sentence + 3 signals tell the operator where the deal stands. The primary action card surfaces the next move directly — no need to drill through tabs to find it. Tabs sit beneath as lenses into the deal's history and artifacts. The 80% scan case (what do I need to do with this deal?) is answered above the tab strip.
What this surface refuses to be Not a Salesforce opportunity detail. Not a HubSpot deal record. Not five mini-dashboards stitched together. Not a custom-field intake form. F-SL3 is the deal lens with one prominent next-action card and tabbed drill underneath. Proposal lives inside the deal, not parallel to it.
