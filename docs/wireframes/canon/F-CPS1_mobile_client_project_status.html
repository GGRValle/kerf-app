Canon distinction · proposal vs contract vs CO

  • Proposal · unsigned · pre-contract · revisions modify it · client previews via F-PV1
  • Contract · signed proposal · post-signing · change orders modify it · client status views via F-CPS1 (this surface)
  • Change order · scope modification to an executed contract · CO previews via F-B1c (CO-level only)
  • F-CPS1 exists because clients sometimes need to see the current project status (contract + pending COs together) without the contractor sending a separate document for each piece

Dark chrome · light paper · ALWAYS

  • clientPhone chrome theme-aware (dark by default, flips with toggle)
  • clientPaper artifact stays light regardless of theme · per F-B1c convention · client artifacts always render on light paper because that's what gets sent/printed
  • Paper has its own color tokens (--paper-*) that don't flip with theme
  • Even in dark mode, the operator sees a light document inside their dark phone chrome — accurate preview of what client receives

Operator controls (not visible to client)

Thin band above paper · marked "Operator controls (not visible to client)":

  • Itemization segmented control: Detailed (every line) / Summary (this view · phase totals) / Lump sum (single total only). Operator chooses the granularity the client receives.
  • Show margin toggle: OFF by default = client view. ON = operator-only annotation showing margin per line (small italic text under each amount). Toggle clearly labeled so operator never accidentally sends a margin-visible version.

Paper structure (what client actually sees)

  • Letterhead · GGR brand · CSLB license · contact
  • Proposal title block · "Pantry Conversion · Hernandez Residence" · revision number · proposal # · date
  • Scope of work · 6 phase categories with amounts (in summary itemization · would be more granular in Detailed)
  • CO-002 callout · transparent revision notice · client sees what changed and why (the field walk that surfaced the expansion)
  • Investment summary · base + revision + total · the headline numbers
  • Allowances · cabinetry / tile / hardware
  • Exclusions · 4 items in plain English
  • Terms · payment schedule · timeline · warranty
  • Built-from footer · "contractor-reviewed and approved · prepared with Kerf · numbers reflect Christian's review of past similar projects, today's field measurements, and direct conversation with Maria"

Client-facing AI disclosure rule

  • canonPer kerf_ai_disclosure_pattern.md §13 · client artifacts state "contractor-reviewed and approved", never "AI-assisted by Kerf Right Hand"
  • canonThe contractor (Christian) is the accountable party · Kerf is the tool · disclosure reflects accountability
  • canon"Prepared with Kerf" footer is the only Kerf brand on the artifact — quiet attribution, not co-author
  • canonNo provider attribution (no "Powered by Llama" or "Built with Claude")
  • canon"Numbers reflect Christian's review of past similar projects, today's field measurements, and direct conversation with Maria" — translates the Field → Past Jobs → Pricing continuity into client-facing English without exposing internal mechanics

Continuity made client-readable

The same Field → Scope → Money chain that's visible to the operator at F-PR2 ("Built from: field walk → transcript → priced against your past 11 pantry/cabinetry jobs") gets re-rendered for the client at F-PV1 as plain, trustworthy English:

"Numbers reflect Christian's review of past similar projects, today's field measurements, and direct conversation with Maria."

  • Past similar projects = the cohort comparable
  • Today's field measurements = the RoomPlan scan from F-RC1
  • Direct conversation with Maria = the transcript from F-F1 (exception-tier · the operator's accountability comes through)

The client sees the substance of the trust chain without seeing the mechanics. That's the doctrine: load-bearing in system, lightweight in experience.

  • Per the Decision Card canon: approving on F-B1/B2 commits scope/pricing/terms; external send is a separate gate (here)

  • A Kerf-branded artifact (the artifact is GGR-branded · Kerf is the tool · "prepared with Kerf" is the only attribution)
  • A surface that exposes margin to the client (margin toggle defaults OFF · clearly labeled operator-only)
