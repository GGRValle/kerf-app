What happens in the background as you talk

One conductor (Right Hand, frontier tier) over nine specialists (platform tier) working in parallel. Reference: _docs/architecture/RightHand_Background_Orchestration_Choreography.md.

◈ The principle everything serves
The system fills in silently and only interrupts you for the rare thing that's both high-consequence AND genuinely uncertain. Everything else you review later at the artifact — never mid-sentence. Permissive on the way in; strict only when something becomes real.
1
You talk → Right Hand curates in real time
RH doesn't transcribe — it turns speech into structured reality (client · address · scope · quantities) and tags every value with a confidence level + a routing envelope. Inferring, not interrogating.
↓
2
The curated signal fans out — parallel, background, silent
The routing envelope names which domains each item touches; one utterance lands in many lanes at once. Each works only its slice. None talk to you.

3
Each specialist fills its artifact — trust hierarchy
Per value, ranked source of truth. A missing/uncertain value becomes a visible flag on the draft, not a question to you.
company data fill silently (highest trust) model knowledge backfill, labeled allowance never asserted as fact TBD flagged placeholder

4
Confidence × consequence gate — what surfaces vs what stays a flag
Per value: how sure am I × how much does getting it wrong cost? (This is Framework 002 · Attention Allocation, applied at emit time.)
✓ Fill silently / soft-flag
High confidence or low consequence. The dozen ordinary unknowns. No interruption — flooring species → allowance + flag.
! Surface — ONE question
Low confidence AND high consequence only. e.g. "is the kitchen staying in its footprint?" — swings the number, genuinely unknown. The rare interrupt worth your flow.
↓
5
Right Hand conducts — you stay in the conversation
RH surfaces the one thing that needs you, vouches for the rest, never relays nine agents' status. The agents keep building as you talk — so by "take me there," the estimate is already assembled, flags marking the soft spots.
↓
6
You review at the artifact — not in the stream
On the estimate page: source-labeled lines, flagged assumptions, TBDs visible. You scan a finished draft and correct only the exceptions ("bump the cabinet allowance, flooring's white oak") → the estimating agent updates. Never quizzed line by line.
↓
⛔ The only hard stop — the consequence gate
Filing the lead · sending to the client · anything touching money → one confirmation, not a quiz. "File this as a lead for Rodriguez?" → Yes → done.

It earns its silence: every flag you correct teaches that lane the tenant's real numbers (tenant-private CRAFT) → next job, fewer guesses, fewer flags, less to review. Better data → less interruption. — Runtime note: today only the conductor is wired (Sprint E ≈ 4 tools, request/response); the nine background agents aren't instantiated, which is why "build it" currently loops. The build stands up estimating first on this exact pattern, then generalizes.
