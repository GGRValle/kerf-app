F-DL3 · Field guidance level · how hard it pushes is your call

The setting behind F-DL2. You decide how much structure the crew feels — because getting them to use it is step one, and a tool that feels like a tracking anklet doesn't get used. Three levels: Light touch (the default), Standard, Strict. They change what's required to clock out, how loud the nudges are, what routes to managers, and even the tone of the copy. Location and hours are always manager-side — the level decides how much, never whether the crew gets "watched."

F-DL3 · Spec

Three levels

  • Light touch (default): note encouraged; no location to crew; private buzz; managers alerted only on a help request. Adoption-first.
  • Standard: the three musts required; location manager-side; over-hours → super; super approves.
  • Strict: musts + geofence + hours-window enforced and shown; check-in required to continue; every exception audited.

What scales

  • Clock-out requirements · whether the worker sees location · nudge enforcement · what routes to managers · copy tone.

Invariants (all levels)

  • Location/hours are manager signals, not crew-facing surveillance — until Strict, opted into deliberately.
  • Right Hand routes to a human; it never auto-disciplines.
  • Sits in Settings → Field & crew; owner/admin permission. Per-tenant; could later be per-crew or per-job.

Why it exists

  • Adoption is step one. The owner sizes the pressure; the product defaults gentle (D-048 daily-simple).
Start gentle, tighten laterDefault is Light — get the crew comfortable, then turn the dial up only where a job or payroll rule demands it. The product never assumes maximum surveillance.
One dial, many surfacesThis level drives F-DL2's musts, nudge, copy tone, and routing — and any future field surface. Change it once; the whole field experience adjusts.
