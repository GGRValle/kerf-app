F-DL2 · Field Hand clock-out · warm, not watched

Reworked per founder feedback. The crew member's clock-out turns on three musts they control — daily note · cleaned up your work area · submitted pictures — not on surveillance. There's no "we tracked you on GPS all day" line in the worker's view; location and over-hours are manager-side signals, surfaced to the super, not waved in the worker's face. A caring mid-day buzz at ~8 hours invites a check-in rather than scolding. And how hard any of this pushes is owner-configurable (F-DL3) — because getting the crew to use it at all is step one.

A · Mid-day buzz

Look out for, don't watch

  • Fires as a phone buzz near the day's hour line. Copy invites a check-in ("Message Ana / keep going"), never "you're over."
  • "Nobody's been pinged" — the nudge is private to the worker first; escalation only if they blow past + the level allows.
  • Playful tone ("slow down there, cowboy") is the Light/Standard voice; Strict swaps to plain. Tone scales with F-DL3.

B · The three musts

What clock-out turns on

  • Daily note · Cleaned up work area · Submitted pictures. The founder's must-haves — all things the worker controls.
  • No surveillance line. "On-site / GPS matched" is gone from the worker's view.
  • Hours show as a quiet footnote ("7h 42m"), not a compliance gate.
  • "Ana approves it from here" — the worker sees where it goes, plainly.

C · Not yet + manager side

Friendlier block

  • "Hold up — two things before you go" + the fastest fix per item. Never "Can't clock out."

Location/hours live here

  • Over-window + OT are manager-side signals (Ana / Admin by rule), not the worker's checklist.
  • Super's queue: Approve / Send back. Nothing auto-approves (D-053 two-artifact, audited).
  • What routes, and how loud, is set in F-DL3 guidance level.
