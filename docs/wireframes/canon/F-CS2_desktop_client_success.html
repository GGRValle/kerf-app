Success-lens vs canonical record

F-CS1/CS2 is the success-lens projection of the client entity — focused narrowly on relationship / marketing / repeat-business state. F-CL5/CL6 (canonical record) carries the full ledger — all projects, all AR/AP, all comms, all files. Same underlying client, different lens. The Watching card's "Open canonical client record" CTA routes from success view to the full record.

Six operator questions answered above-the-fold

  • Where are they in the lifecycle? Header advocate chip + 5-step horizontal stepper (with amounts on completed stages)
  • How fresh is the relationship? Touch cadence section (last · cadence + on-track state · next · after that)
  • Are they advocating? Advocacy state section (reviews · referrals · worth · status)
  • What action is open right now? Open marketing actions · 3 cards with state chips and per-card CTA
  • How happy are they? NPS history · per-project rows + trend line
  • What's Right Hand watching? Watching rail card · 4 monitors + canonical-record drill

Grid doctrine applied

  • DETAILThis is a detail surface — single-client success depth. No grid view. No view-mode toggle.
  • Single-frame wireframe per the 4-question dispatch
  • No tabs · Success is a focused single-lens projection (vs F-CL6's six lenses)

Disciplines applied

  • D1Business brain, not file cabinet — Synthesis sections render first. Individual messages live in canonical record Comms tab; full project history in canonical record Projects tab.
  • D2Red is chip-tier only — no full-row red borders anywhere on this advocate-stage render. State chips stay amber/cyan/muted. Red reserved for genuine risk (none here).
  • D3No agent names in operator copy — "anniversary drafted" / "queued by cadence engine" — name the work, not the agent. Right Hand named only on RH synthesis + Watching card.
  • D4Translate canon vocab — "advocate status," "top quintile," "your book," "LTV multiplier," "stable-high" — plainspoken English. No "lifecycle segment 4," "cohort routing envelope," "AltitudePacket."
  • D5Sidebar IA — Marketing expanded with sub-items (Reviews & Referrals · Outreach · Attribution · Success). Success is the active sub-item.

Sidebar IA · Marketing expansion

Per Operating Model Canon, Marketing is one of the 9 business graph domains; under Marketing sit three pillars + Client Success. F-MK1/MK2 introduced Marketing as a single top-level item with no sub-items rendered. F-CS2 expands it to surface the 4 marketing sub-domains per current canon: Reviews & Referrals · Outreach · Attribution · Success. Success is active (this surface lives under it). The Marketing parent item carries the expanded visual state — softer amber background, no full active border. Pattern mirrors the Team & Ops → Clients precedent.

Main column ordering

  • Lifecycle stage · full-width horizontal stepper · 5 stages, current dot highlighted
  • Touch cadence + Advocacy state · two-column paired row · same row height, glanceable comparison
  • Open marketing actions · stacked action cards with per-card CTA
  • NPS history · per-project NPS rows + trend summary

Rationale: lifecycle answers "where?" Cadence + Advocacy answer "how fresh? how strong?" together at equal weight. Actions answer "what do I do?" NPS answers "how happy?" Each section is one operator question.

  • Recent touches · last 3 comms summary only · drill to canonical Comms tab for full thread
